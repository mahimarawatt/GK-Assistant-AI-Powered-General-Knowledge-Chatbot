The New 7 Wonders of the World represent iconic feats of engineering and cultural significance across the globe. Here is the list with a brief explanation of each:
Great Wall of China (China): Built over many centuries starting in the 7th century BCE, this massive series of fortifications was designed to protect the Chinese states and empire from nomadic invasions. It stretches over 13,000 miles across diverse terrain.
Petra (Jordan): Often called the "Rose City" due to the color of the stone, Petra was the capital of the Nabataean Kingdom around 300 BCE. Its most famous feature is Al-Khazneh (The Treasury), a massive temple facade carved directly into a sandstone cliff face.
The Colosseum (Italy): Located in the heart of Rome, this oval amphitheatre was completed in 80 CE. It is the largest ancient amphitheatre ever built and was used for gladiatorial contests, public spectacles, and dramas for audiences of up to 80,000 people.
Chichén Itzá (Mexico): A major focal point in the Northern Maya Lowlands, this archaeological site features the Temple of Kukulcán (El Castillo). The pyramid is designed so that during equinoxes, the sun creates the shadow of a serpent crawling down the stairs.
Machu Picchu (Peru): This 15th-century Inca citadel is located on a mountain ridge 7,970 feet above sea level. It is renowned for its sophisticated dry-stone walls that fuse huge blocks without the use of mortar and its panoramic views of the Andes.
Taj Mahal (India): An immense mausoleum of white marble, built in Agra between 1631 and 1648 by order of the Mughal emperor Shah Jahan in memory of his favorite wife. It is considered the jewel of Muslim art in India and a masterpiece of world heritage.
Christ the Redeemer (Brazil): This Art Deco statue of Jesus Christ stands 98 feet tall with arms stretching 92 feet wide. Completed in 1931, it sits atop Mount Corcovado overlooking Rio de Janeiro and has become a global symbol of Christianity and peace.
Honorary Status: The Great Pyramid of Giza in Egypt, the only surviving member of the original Seven Wonders of the Ancient World, was granted honorary status alongside these seven.
